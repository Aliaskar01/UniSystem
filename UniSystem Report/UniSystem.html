University System Documentation

This documentation provides an overview of the key classes in the university system project, including their purpose, fields, and methods.

Admin

The Admin class represents an administrator in the university system who has elevated permissions to manage resources.

Fields:

  • id: Unique identifier for the admin.
  • name: Name of the admin.
  • email: Contact email of the admin.

Methods:

  • addUser(): Adds a new user to the system.
  • removeUser(): Removes an existing user.

Employee

The Employee class represents a general employee in the university, such as teachers or support staff.

Fields:

  • id: Unique identifier for the employee.
  • name: Name of the employee.
  • position: Role or job title of the employee.

Methods:

  • work(): Defines the work performed by the employee.
  • report(): Generates a report of activities.

Manager

The Manager class represents a manager responsible for overseeing departments or projects.

Fields:

  • id: Unique identifier for the manager.
  • department: The department managed by the individual.

Methods:

  • assignTask(): Assigns tasks to employees.
  • reviewPerformance(): Reviews the performance of employees.

Researcher

The Researcher class represents a university researcher involved in academic projects and publications.

Fields:

  • id: Unique identifier for the researcher.
  • specialization: Area of research specialization.

Methods:

  • publishPaper(): Publishes a research paper.
  • conductExperiment(): Conducts an experiment.

Student

The Student class represents a student enrolled at the university.

Fields:

  • id: Unique identifier for the student.
  • name: Name of the student.
  • major: The student's field of study.

Methods:

  • enroll(): Enrolls the student in a course.
  • submitAssignment(): Submits an assignment for grading.

Course

The Course class represents a university course that students can enroll in.

Fields:

  • code: Unique course code.
  • title: Title of the course.
  • credits: Number of credits awarded for the course.

Methods:

  • addStudent(): Adds a student to the course.
  • removeStudent(): Removes a student from the course.

ResearchPaper

The ResearchPaper class represents an academic paper authored by researchers.

Fields:

  • title: Title of the research paper.
  • author: Author(s) of the paper.
  • journal: The journal where the paper is published.

Methods:

  • submit(): Submits the paper for review.
  • getCitation(): Generates a citation for the paper.
